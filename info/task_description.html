Related to the mission, you may be interested at Catalan’s conjecture, these days a proven mathematical theorem that says that after the special case of the two consecutive perfect powers 8 and 9, whenever a positive integer n is a perfect power, n – 1 is never a perfect power. For example, we don’t have to slog through all potential ways to express the number as an integer power to know from the get-go that 12345**67890-1 is not a perfect power. This also illustrates the common asymmetry between performing a computation to opposite directions. Given some big chungus integer such as 4922235242952026704037113243122008064, but not the formula that originally produced it, it is not quite easy to tell whether that integer is a perfect power, or some perfect power plus minus one.
